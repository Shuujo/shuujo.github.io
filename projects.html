✦ Kinjo Sushi & Grill

Work Project - Bubble Tea Designs

✦ In the summer of 2024, I was working at the Calgary-based Kinjo Sushi & Grill restaurant, as a server and bartender. I was approached by the management staff, as they were aware I was passionate about graphic design and illustration, and they granted me the opportunity to create new bubble tea designs for the company.

✦ I was able to produce one main design that would be displayed on the side of the cup, and several designs that would be displayed on top of the cup. I was given a limited colour palette, however I did want to display the wide arrange of colours of the bubble tea, so I ended up incorporating some clear elements in my designs as I wanted those bubble tea colours to shine too.

SunPeel ✦

School Project - Brand Redesign

✦ In this project, we were given a brand that was created from past students and tasked with creating a campaign that suited to that company's needs.

✦ SunPeel is a local fruit brand that focuses on delivering fresh oranges to surrounding customers. However, the old SunPeel was lacking in customer engagement as their old logo was deemed 'outdated'. Our group was tasked with redesigning the brand and creating social media advertisements to help boost awareness and give SunPeel a fresh, modern look.

✦ The Odyssey [ + Express]

School Project - SAITSA: The Odyssey

✦ The Odyssey is a student-driven business located at the Southern Alberta Institue of Technology. Powered by Saitsa (SAIT Student Association), customers can purchase meals, drinks, and snacks, with all purchases going directly back to funding more student-led events.

✦ In this project, we were to choose any brand that was related to SAIT and create a motion graphic poster.

✦ I chose The Odyssey, as I was inspired by the name and chose to represent it with the use of two ships; An easy-going flying ship to represent the main relaxing cafe spot [Odyssey Market], and a hypersonic spaceship, made to represent the smaller, more convenient location [Odyssey Express].
